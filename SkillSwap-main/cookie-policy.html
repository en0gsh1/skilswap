Local Storage & Tracking Policy

SkillSwap Learning Platform

Last Updated: December 2024

1. Introduction

This Local Storage & Tracking Policy explains how SkillSwap uses browser local storage, session storage, and other tracking technologies to enhance your learning experience. While we don't use traditional cookies, we do store information locally on your device and track certain user metrics.

Important Note: We use local storage instead of cookies to store your preferences and session data. This information stays on your device and helps us provide a better user experience.

2. What We Store Locally

We use your browser's local storage to save information that enhances your experience on our platform:

Storage Type Purpose Data Stored Duration
Essential User Authentication Login tokens, session data Until logout
Functional Theme Preferences Dark/light mode setting Persistent
Functional Language Settings Selected language preference Persistent
Functional Course Progress Completed lessons, bookmarks Until cleared
Analytics Usage Tracking Visit timestamps, page views 30 days
Functional Form Data Draft submissions, preferences Until submitted

3. User Tracking and Analytics

We collect anonymized analytics data to improve our platform and understand user behavior. This tracking does NOT include:

  • Exact Location: We do not track your precise geographic location
  • Personal Identification: Analytics data is aggregated and anonymized
  • Cross-Site Tracking: We only track activity on our platform

What we DO track:

  • User Count: Total number of active users on the platform
  • Subscription Data: Plan types, upgrade patterns (anonymized)
  • Daily Activity: Login frequency, course engagement, feature usage
  • Platform Performance: Page load times, error rates, system performance
  • Learning Progress: Course completion rates, skill development metrics
  • General Location: Country/region for service optimization (not exact location)

5. How Long We Store Data

Different types of locally stored data have different retention periods:

  • Session Data: Cleared when you log out or close your browser
  • Preferences: Stored until you manually clear them or change settings
  • Course Progress: Stored until you complete the course or clear your data
  • Analytics Data: Automatically cleared after 30 days
  • Cached Content: Cleared periodically or when storage limits are reached

6. Managing Your Local Storage

You have full control over the data stored locally on your device:

Browser-Level Controls:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Site Settings > Storage
  • Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings > Site Permissions > Cookies and Site Data

7. Third-Party Analytics

We may use third-party analytics services to better understand user behavior:

  • Google Analytics: Website traffic and user behavior analysis
  • Performance Monitoring: Error tracking and performance optimization
  • User Feedback Tools: Survey and feedback collection platforms

These services may use their own tracking technologies. You can opt out of Google Analytics by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

8. Mobile App Storage

If you use our mobile application, we may store similar data locally on your device:

  • App preferences and settings
  • Offline course content
  • User authentication data
  • Usage analytics (anonymized)

You can manage this data through your device's app settings or by uninstalling the application.

9. Data Security

We implement security measures to protect locally stored data:

  • Encryption: Sensitive data is encrypted before local storage
  • Access Controls: Data is only accessible by our platform
  • Regular Cleanup: Automatic removal of expired or unnecessary data
  • Secure Transmission: All data transfers use HTTPS encryption

10. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Local Storage & Tracking Policy to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will notify you of significant changes by:

  • Posting an updated policy on our website
  • Sending email notifications to registered users
  • Displaying a notice on our platform
